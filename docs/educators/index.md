# Educator Track

Resources for teaching interactive narrative with VNFramework — whether you're running a one-week jam or a full-semester course.

## Pages

- [Glossary](glossary.md) — VN terms, framework-specific terms, and how each maps onto features your students will use.
- [Curriculum outline](curriculum-outline.md) — a suggested 8–12 week structure that builds VN literacy from zero.
- [Assignment templates](assignment-templates.md) — three ready-to-use assignments: dialogue, branching narrative, custom theme.

## Why use VNFramework in a classroom?

- **No code required.** Students work with DataAssets in the editor's Details panel. The conceptual ramp is "fill in fields and link assets together," not "learn Blueprints or C++."
- **Visible structure.** A `UVNProjectAsset → UVNChapterAsset → UVNSceneAsset` tree is easy to draw on a whiteboard and matches how students think about story structure.
- **Real engine.** Students who graduate to gameplay or 3D work in UE 5 already know the editor.
- **Importer-friendly.** Students who prefer to write in Ink, Yarn, or a spreadsheet can author there and import.
